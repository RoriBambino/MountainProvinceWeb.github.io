Tadian

Tadian, officially the Municipality of Tadian is a 4th class municipality in the province of Mountain Province, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 19,341 people.

Tadian was formerly known as Kayan. In 1957, the seat of government was transferred to the barrio of Tadian. Two years later the town was renamed to Tadian.

Municipalities

The province is a lone district comprised of 10 municipalities and 144 barangays. The municipalities are Sadanga, Natonin, Paracelis, Barlig, Bontoc, Besao, Sagada, Bauko, Tadian, and Sabangan.

Ayyoweng de Lambak ed Tadian

First Friday and Saturday of March

Ayyoweng di Lambak ed Tadian (a celebration and thanksgiving), is a subtle and a powerful medium through which “i-Tadian” communicate their varied emotions to the outside world.

Mt. Mog-ao

Mt. Mog-ao stands majestically amidst the rolling plains, making it one of the popular tourist spots in Tadian, Mountain Province. It is a visible landmark in almost every barangay. While on top, one can glimpse of the breathtaking views of Sunny Side, adjacent Bauko, and the municipalities of Quirino and Cervantes in Ilocos Sur province.

Gawaan Lake
