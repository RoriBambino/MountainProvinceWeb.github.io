February - Last Week

Ubaya Festival, Besao

A cultural celebration observed by every iBesao community to commemorate a transition in their agricultural calendar. Thus, after the planting season, the community rests and celebrates to mark the end of their farm work. It ushers in the start of another major community activity e.g. house repair or construction. Likewise after harvest time, the community again celebrates the UBAYA to express their thanks for their blessings.

June 17-18

Fortan Festival, Barlig

“Fortan Festival,” games like tug-of-war are given local flavor, not only because they are christened with names like “eenat” and “sangkor” respectively, but because the participants, both men and women, done their traditional costumes of intricately woven G-strings or “bahag” in vibrant red, black, and white thread.Children also participate in “fiawat,” “chinaray” or “sus-uwat” ball games.

June - Last Week, Every after 2 Years

Fvegnash Festival, Sadanga

Residents coming from the eight villages of this town will come together to celebrate showcasing their rich culture, traditions and arts handed down to them by their forefathers. On June 24, activities include indigenous games (contested games) like shanggor, ag-agto, ammadang, ineta-an and gug-uyod; indigenous fun games like av-ava and shap-shapaak/pak-pakaong/yakyakingking; indigenous handicrafts such as ragan na saknong and tugnev na botelya.
